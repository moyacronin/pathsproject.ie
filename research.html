Methodology & findings

Our research

We follow a trauma-informed, human-centred approach to understanding housing and service access for Traveller community members living with disabilities.

Methodology

Trauma-informed, human-centred research

PATHS is built around methods that prioritise trust, safety, and meaningful collaboration at every stage, understanding the history of discrimination and institutional harm many Traveller families have experienced.

Safety & trust first

Sessions are designed to give participants complete control over what is shared, with flexible pacing, and no pressure to disclose sensitive information.

Human-centred design

Workshops and interviews are flexible, blended between online and in-person interaction, structured around participants' own priorities and use plain language.

Community partnership

Traveller-led organisations have aided in shaping the research, recruiting participants, and reviewing resources and findings before they are made public.

Ethics & consent

PATHS has received ethical approval from MTU's Human Research Ethics Committee (Approval No: MTU-HREC-FER-26-009-A). All participants take part on the basis of informed consent: before any workshop, interview, or session, we explain what's involved, how the information will be used, and who will see it.
