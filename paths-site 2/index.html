About the project

Housing that works starts with who it's built for.

Members of the Traveller community continue to face significant barriers to safe, appropriate, and accessible accommodation across Ireland — barriers that are often compounded for those who are also disabled or have additional support needs. PATHS was set up to understand these barriers directly from the people experiencing them, and to work alongside Traveller-led organisations, service providers, and local authorities to identify practical, achievable improvements.

The project combines qualitative research with participatory and co-design methods: workshops, interviews, and collaborative sessions that put lived experience at the centre of how findings and recommendations are shaped.

Our approach

Research done with the community, not on it

Community-led

Traveller organisations and community members shape the research questions, the methods, and how findings get used — not just the data collection.

Evidence-based

Findings are grounded in workshops, interviews, and site visits, and cross-checked against housing policy and service delivery in practice.

Built for action

Outputs are written for people who can act on them — local authorities, housing bodies, and service providers — alongside the community itself.
